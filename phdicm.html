Program Description The Ph.D. program provides students maximum flexibility to tailor their education to topical and regional interests and the demands of the global job market. All students complete the following program components (full curriculum and course descriptions are available at http://phd.hss.kennesaw.edu/program/): Core Seminars: The three core seminars provide students with a thorough grounding in the fundamental concepts, the ethical and cultural foundations and practical application in international conflict management and resolution. Research Methods: Preparation in research methods includes courses in quantitative methods, qualitative methods, and research design and electives in international policy analysis, program evaluation and advanced qualitative and quantitative methods. International Experience: Students practice their conflict management skills in an applied international setting and gain an understanding of today’s complex challenges marked by globalization, the impact of technology, competition for scarce resources, and deepening interconnections between people and institutions. Related Studies: Students complete a number of related studies courses on a theme or topic of their choice to establish a solid foundation and specialization for dissertation and future research. Program Curricular Electives: Electives include courses focused on critical issues in conflict management, such as: methods of reconciliation; human rights and the rule of law; the role of the media in conflict; identity, race, and ethnicity; the role of third parties and international organizations; conflict prevention and transformation; peace-building and reconciliation; sustainable development and post-conflict reconstruction. Foreign Language: The program requires professional competency in a language in addition to English. Research and Teaching Assistantships, including tuition remission and a monthly stipend, are available to qualifying students.
